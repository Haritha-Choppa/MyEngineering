Aerospace Engineering



What is Aerospace Engineering?


Aerospace Engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with the design, development, testing, and maintenance of aircraft, spacecraft, and related systems.

It is divided into two main areas:

  1. Aeronautical Engineering– Focuses on aircraft operating within Earth's atmosphere.

  2. Astronautical Engineering– Deals with spacecraft and systems that operate outside Earth's atmosphere.

Core Subjects in Aerospace Engineering:


  • Aerodynamics

  • Aircraft Structures

  • Propulsion Systems

  • Flight Mechanics

  • Avionics

  • Space Dynamics

  • Thermodynamics and Fluid Mechanics

  • Aircraft Materials and Manufacturing

  • Control Systems and Navigation

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

    • Career Opportunities After Aerospace Engineering


    1. IT Sector Jobs (With Additional Skills)


    Though not a traditional IT branch, aerospace engineers can enter the IT sector, especially in domains like simulation, modeling, and software development.


    IT Roles:


    • Software Developer / Programmer

    • Simulation & Modeling Engineer (ANSYS, MATLAB)

    • Data Analyst / Engineer

    • Aerospace Software Engineer

    • Embedded Systems Developer (in avionics)

      • Skills Needed:


      -> Programming (Python, C/C++, MATLAB)

      -> Simulation Tools (ANSYS, CFD, Simulink)

      -> Embedded systems and control programming

      2. Non-IT Sector Jobs (Core Aerospace Roles)


      These roles involve working with aircraft design, systems, testing, and maintenance in core aerospace and defense sectors.


      Core Roles:


      • Aerodynamics Engineer

      • Aerospace Design Engineer

      • Flight Test Engineer

      • Structural Analyst

      • Propulsion Engineer

      • UAV/Drone Developer

      • Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (after AME license)

          • Industries:


          Aerospace Manufacturing (Boeing, Airbus, Safran)

          Defense (HAL, DRDO, BEL)

          Airlines (IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet – for maintenance roles)

          Space Research (ISRO, NASA – for R&D roles)

          3. Government Sector Jobs


          There is strong government demand in research, defense, and aviation-related departments for aerospace engineers.

          A. Technical Government Jobs:


          -> ISRO / DRDO / HAL / BHEL / BEL – Scientist/Engineer roles

          -> Airports Authority of India (AAI) – ATC & Engineering services

          -> Indian Air Force (IAF) – Aeronautical engineers, technical branch

          -> Civil Aviation Department – Regulatory & engineering roles

          B. General Government Jobs:


          -> UPSC Civil Services – IAS, IPS, IFS

          -> SSC CGL / CHSL – Central government roles

          -> Banking – IBPS, SBI PO, RBI

          -> Defense – AFCAT, CDS (engineering & logistics roles)

          Aerospace Engineering is a specialized and high-tech field, ideal for those interested in aircraft, spacecraft, and aviation systems. While core jobs are competitive, opportunities are rewarding—especially in R&D, defense, and space sectors. Upskilling in software and simulations also opens doors to IT or hybrid tech roles.
