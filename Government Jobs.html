-->ISRO, DRDO, BARC, NIC – Software Engineer, Research Scientist

-->Digital India, UIDAI, NIC – Tech Support, Developers



B. Non-Technical Government Jobs:


-->Civil Services (IAS/IPS/IFS) – Through UPSC

-->Banking Jobs – IBPS PO, SBI PO, Clerk

-->SSC CGL, CHSL – Officer and assistant level positions

-->Railway, Defense Services – NDA, CDS, AFCAT (non-IT roles too)


Mechanical Engineering

3. Government Sector Jobs


Mechanical engineers have strong demand in various public sector units (PSUs) and central/state government departments.

A. Core Government Jobs (Technical):


--> PSUs: BHEL, GAIL, NTPC, ONGC, IOCL (through GATE)

--> Indian Railways – RRB JE/SSE

--> ISRO / DRDO / BARC – Scientist/Engineer roles

--> Indian Navy & Air Force – Technical Officers

--> IES (Indian Engineering Services) – Through UPSC ESE Exam

--> State Public Service Commissions – AE/JE posts

B. Non-Technical Government Jobs:


--> UPSC Civil Services – IAS, IPS, IFS

--> SSC CGL / CHSL – Govt clerical and officer roles

--> Banking – IBPS PO, SBI PO, RBI Grade B

--> Defense – CDS, AFCAT (Logistics, Technical roles)


Civil Engineering

3. Government Sector Jobs


Civil Engineering has excellent scope in government departments, especially related to infrastructure, water, roads, and urban development.

A. Core Government Jobs:


-> Indian Engineering Services (IES) – via UPSC

-> State Public Works Department (PWD) – AE/JE posts

-> Indian Railways – RRB JE/SSE (Civil)

-> CPWD, MES, BRO – Civil Engineers

-> Urban Local Bodies / Municipalities

-> PSUs (BHEL, NTPC, ONGC, etc.) – Through GATE

-> Water Boards / Irrigation Departments


B. Non-Core Government Jobs:


-> UPSC (IAS, IPS, IFS) – Civil Services

-> SSC JE – Junior Engineer (Civil)

-> Banking – IBPS PO, SBI PO, RBI

-> Defense – CDS, AFCAT (Logistics, Tech roles)


Electrical,Electronical and Engineering

3. Government Sector Jobs


EEE graduates are eligible for a wide range of core and general government jobs, especially in power and energy sectors.


A. Core Government Jobs:


-> PSUs (via GATE): NTPC, BHEL, ONGC, GAIL, PGCIL

-> Indian Railways: JE/SSE (Electrical)

-> Electricity Boards / State Transmission Utilities

-> ISRO, DRDO, BARC: Scientific and engineering roles

-> IES (Indian Engineering Services): Electrical stream


B. Non-Core Government Jobs:


-> UPSC (IAS, IPS, IFS)

-> SSC CGL / JE (Electrical)

-> Banking: IBPS, SBI PO, RBI Grade B

-> Defense: AFCAT, CDS (technical/logistics roles)

      Cyber Security

      3. Government Sector


      The government requires strong cybersecurity professionals to protect national digital assets and infrastructure.

      Opportunities:


      -> DRDO, ISRO, NIC, CERT-In – Cyber defense, national security

      -> Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) – Cybercrime investigation

      -> National Critical Information Infrastructure Protection Centre (NCIIPC)

      -> Defense Cyber Agencies

      -> Public sector banks and insurance companies

      Exams: GATE (for NIC, DRDO), SSC, UPSC (for technical roles), and direct recruitment by agencies


      Cybersecurity is a high-growth, high-demand field with global importance. It offers strong job security, evolving career paths, and impactful work. With rising digital threats, skilled cybersecurity professionals are essential in both the private and public sectors.
